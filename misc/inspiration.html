inspiration

this whole site is a love letter to omniglot.

i grew up using simon ager's site pretty much every day since i was 12. it was the very thing that made me fall in love with scripts, minority languages, and how diverse linguistics can get. without omniglot i probably wouldnt be making anything like this.

kalbarchyvas originally started as an idea of me just wanting my own personal archive that blends the best of both worlds of omniglot and wikipedia, with an omniglot base and a wikipedia level of information. when i finally began to start making it, tho, it turned into something that felt just like omniglot with my own extra depth. a lot of the info on this site is still straight from omniglot and then double-checked or expanded on with other sources.

big respect to simon ager for running the original for 25+ years by himself. that stuff is legendary. if you're on this website right now and don't use omniglot, i advise you to at least check it out for a few hours. there's so much information right in your hands and i can even say the site brings me memories. i was so dedicated to omniglot that i've looked through all 2000+ languages over 7 times in just a few months before. every reason this site exists can be thanks to omniglot.

and just one last thing, i dont want this to be a replacement for omniglot. i love omniglot and id hate to see my own creation take over it, so please, if youre here, just check out omniglot. this is just my own personal language archive that just happens to be public. i made kalbarchyvas to expand on omniglot, not to replace.






thank you simon.
